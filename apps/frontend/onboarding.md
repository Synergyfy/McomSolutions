### 3. The 90-day programme (default sequence)

Henry repeatedly said some businesses may finish in 2 weeks, some in 30–60 days, and some will take the full 90 days. The sequence remains the same; completion speed varies.

### Days 1–7 — Foundation

MCOM Central

* Verify business identity and contact details.

* Upload logo/brand assets (or request assistance).

* Confirm sector/category and opening hours.

* Activate referral profile (247GBS Affiliates — automatic).

* Generate QR/smart links (MCOM QLinks).

* Progress target: 20–30%.

### Days 8–21 — Digital Presence

MCOM Mall

* Create storefront and business description.

* Add products/services, photos, categories, pricing.

* Publish initial offers/promotions.

* Progress target: 40–50%.

### Days 22–35 — Customer Retention

MCOM Rewards

* Create points/rewards programme.

* Configure welcome offer and customer benefits.

* Connect QR links to rewards journeys.

* Progress target: 55–65%.

### Days 36–49 — Engagement & Capture

Spin · Hotspot

* Create MCOM Spin campaign.

* Configure Hotspot/Wi-Fi customer capture if applicable.

* Test customer journeys and data capture.

* Progress target: 65–75%.

### Days 50–63 — Network & Visibility

Affiliates · Expo

* Invite other businesses via referral tools.

* Join borough/community activities and leaderboard challenges.

* Prepare Expo/networking profile if eligible.

* Progress target: 75–85%.

### Days 64–90 — Audit Readiness & Audit

247GBS Audit

* Ensure profile, storefront, rewards, and assets are complete.

* Invite accountant if needed for financial sections.

* Complete the sector-specific audit.

* Review → Submit → Analysis → Executive Summary → Recommendations.

* Progress target: 100%.

### 6. Fast-track and admin controls

* Fast-track: Admin can mark tasks complete, import existing data, and unlock the audit early. A prepared business may reach 100% in ~2 weeks.

* Standard: Default 90-day sequence and pacing.

* Paused/Extended: Admin can pause, extend beyond 90 days, or reassign support.

* Readiness gates: Audit, Expo, campaign publishing, and advanced features can require minimum completion thresholds and verification status.
